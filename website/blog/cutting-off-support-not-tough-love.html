Cutting off welfare for 18-19 year-olds is not just "tough love" - it's neglect

Posted By Oscar on 2025

On the 5th of October, the government announced that 18-19-year-olds whose parents have a combined annual income of $65,000 per year will be unable to access the Jobseeker Benefit. The changes are estimated to affect 4,300 New Zealanders.

In a press conference , Christopher Luxon called it "a bit of tough love", but labelling it as such does not make it true. For too many New Zealanders, this move is a step towards financial insecurity.

Consider this: the median annual wage per person is about $70,000 per year. By setting the combined threshold at less than the median wage per parent, this policy will exclude thousands of families who are by no means wealthy - and who may already struggle to support themselves.

For thousands of New Zealanders, this is not "tough love". It's a sudden shove into financial insecurity. Not all families with an income of over $65,000 per year can - or will - support their children. Many young people already juggle responsibilities. Stripping them of welfare does not teach responsibility. Rather, it forces them to survive on luck, charity or underpaid work.

This is not about "tough love". It's about ignoring the realities of life in New Zealand. It's a policy that will hurt those whom it claims to protect.

If the government truly cared about empowering young adults, it would provide actual investment in education, training and support services - not cut a lifeline for those trying to stand on their own two feet.

Calling this "tough love' is a convenient excuse for a policy that is reckless. Young people deserve guidance, not abandonment and neglect.

I hope to have access to support services in the current years, but with the government's current attitude to policy, hope alone will not be enough.
