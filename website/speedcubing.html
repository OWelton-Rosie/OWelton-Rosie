Speedcubing

Speedcubing (also known as cubing or speedsolving) is the activity of competitively solving Rubik's Cube-like puzzles in the shortest possible time. It involves a combination of pattern recognition, finger dexterity, and memory skills.

Global speedcubing is governed by the World Cube Association (WCA).

The current world record for a single solve of a 3x3x3 cube is 3.13 seconds and is held by the United States' Max Park.
