Speedcubing

What is speedcubing?

Speedcubing (also known as speedsolving or cubing), is the competitive solving of Rubik's Cube-like puzzles. Global speedcubing is sanctioned by the World Cube Association (WCA).

The current world record for a single solve of a 3x3x3 Rubik's Cube is 3.05 seconds and is held by China's Xuanyi Geng.

My speedcubing journey

I have been speedcubing since December 2023, have competed in 10 competitions and completed over 230 solves in WCA-recognised competitions. I am currently a member of the Speedcubing New Zealand Promotional Team and a member of the Speedcubing New Zealand Stream Team.

I use the 4 Look Last Layer method to solve the 3x3x3 Rubik's Cube in under 20 seconds on average. I am currently ranked 72nd in New Zealand for Pyraminx (a 4-sided, pyramind-shaped puzzle) average, with a result of 5.67 seconds. You can see all of the results I have achieved in competitions on my WCA Profile.

Some of my notable speedcubing achievements

  • In early 2024, I was appointed as a member of the Speedcubing New Zealand Promotional Team. I was made a member of the SNZ Stream Team shortly after.
  • Co-organiser of Just Solve in Johnsonville 2025.
  • In March 2025, I set a Pyraminx average of 5.76 seconds, placing me 72nd in New Zealand.
